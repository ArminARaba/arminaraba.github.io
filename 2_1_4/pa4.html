Title

Onboarding assignment

Content

STARR

Situation

In the onboarding assignment, we had to make a video of the university, which should consist of these things: the media industry, some kind of instruction to the university, the city, the course, and the way of thinking. Our team consisted of 6 people including me.

Task

We had to make a film, where we show the mentioned things, so I took the roles of directing, script writing, camera operating and editing.
It was so stressful, but worth it because I learned so many things throughout the time. Even in Premiere Pro which I'm already using for 4 years.

Action

The main concept of this assignment was so great, I almost loved every minute of it, it really gave me the opportunity to expand my creativity, and finally, work with people, which I was dreaming of for a long time. I can say, we were on a really professional level, even the acting and the lightning we did was also great.

Result

I'm so proud of the result, I have never really worked in any filmmaking teams before, where the product was important like here, so I'm so happy about the result. I feel so-so tired, but I know that even though we didn't win the competition in the case, we did our best, which we agree with as a team.
They also admitted the issues we had together, but these were the things that really make us closer to each other.

Reflection

We got 3.5 weeks to done with this, but due to my team, we started the project so late, 10 days before the deadline, which gave me and my teammates so much stress, especially for me, because I knew that if I will not give the best we will not finish the project before the deadline.
But, we did the research together, so there were no problems with that yet, that actually went so great, and even the research doc file was a core, what was the base of everything. 
But, to be honest, I'm way more experienced in filmmaking than any other people were in the project, so I knew what we should do, but due they didn't really want a leader at the beginning of the 3.5 weeks, I tried to not take the role.
Unfortunately, when everything got clearer, I decided to take it, otherwise, we can't finish our project.
So, it sounds a bit bad, because we had to work in a team, but I had to take the script writing, director, cameraman, and editor roles. I would have left it to them because at first I only applied to the cameraman part, but, they were so lazy, and nothing came as the way we expected, so after a little while, with Anni, my fellow Hungarian teammate, we decided to take the chance to direct our team to a better way.

To be honest, I didn't want to make it like a one-man filmmaking project, but I didn't want to fail our first true assignment as a team, so I did my best. Over the last 10 days, I worked more than 70 hours on the project, so it shows that I was really keen on the project finished.

As a team, we already talked about the issues, and they finally recognized what went wrong, so next time, we will hopefully start it earlier, and everyone can get the chance to try out those role, not only me. I mean, I others had other jobs like Anni made the animation, which is amazing, Sinem was also the sound engineer, and she did a really great job in my opinion, but on the way of telling the whole story and making the shots, we should improve, and give more chances to others to try the camera equipment and other stuff as well. I'm only saying it because I had so many issues with it, they thought I was forcing on them my vision. I mean, maybe I did, but my main goal was always to finish with the product so, I didn't care. I also wrote so many scripts, to make everything clear for them, to know what we will shoot in the following days, and so on.
But they didn't manage or cared about it.. so it was very stressful for me to go forward during the project.

Finally, we have done with the project, so it is a great thing, but I would change so many things next time and don't work this much, because it caused me a few health issues, sleeping 3-4 hours during the fourth week.

At the last atelier, we showed the whole film to the class, but when we were showing it, I got shocked by, how boring the product we made was. I mean, I didn't see those issues while I was editing. So next time, hopefully, we will start earlier, so we can get more suggestions in the prototyping part, not just at the end.
Because we got feedback, really great feedback, what I can build on.
So they told us, that the interview was too long, which I can admit also, but my teammates disagreed with me in an earlier part of the editing, so I left it there.
The other thing was about the boring and monotonous narration speech, which I can agree with. 
We also didn't include any humor in the film and the information was too much, which made it even more serious, so.. the first-year students could be scared by this film probably. There was also another suggestion of the interview, they told us that the colors were different. I knew that because I tried to fix it, but because we used two different cameras, a Nikon D750, and a Panasonic Lumix G7 also with two different lenses, I had no idea how to make the two shots look the same, so I tried to play with the colors, so what you see in the video, was the closest option I was able to do. I burnt Martin's face, which was also recognized, but I'm still not a professional, so I'm looking forward to learning more about these in the future.

It would be great, to improve our communication and time management skills with the team, because these were particularly important parts of working in teams.

I have never done such a complex video like this, which shows the timeline as well, you can find it as "timeline_premiere_pro.jpg". I'm so glad full, to be part of this, it really helped me a lot to understand more of the software and improved my editing skills.

So overall I liked the concept of this assignment, it was an amazing opportunity for us, to show our talent in each of those roles, so I'm looking forward to doing other projects like this.
